Graph Theory and Combinatorics

LECTURE NOTES

  1. Lecture 1 Intoduction. Counting Principles. Permutations and Combinations. Binomial and Multinomial Numbers
  2. Lecture 2 Generating Permutations. Ranking and Unranking Permutations. The Pigeonhole Principle. The Inclusion and Exclusion Principle
  3. Lecture 3 Permutations with repetition. Combinations. Enumeration, ranking and unranking algorithms
  4. Lecture 4 The Cycle Structure of Permutations. Advanced Counting Techniques
  5. Lecture 5 Polya theory
  6. Lecture 6 Polya’s enumeration formula. Stirling cycle numbers. Stirling set numbers
  7. Partial exam (written). Lecture 7 Introduction to Graph Theory. Distance in Graphs. Trees
  8. Lecture 8 Kinds of graphs. Data structures for graph representation. Connectivity. The naive algorithm and Warshall algorithm. Bipartite graphs
  9. Lecture 9 Flow Networks. Maximum flow algorithms. Applications and extensions
  10. Lecture 10 Eulerian trails and circuits. Hamiltonian paths and cycles
  11. Lecture 11 Matchings. Definitions. Hall's Theorem and SDRs. Perfect matchings. Spanning trees and minimum spanning trees. Prim's algorithm and Kruskal's algorithm.
  12. Lecture 12 Planar graphs. Graph colorings. Chromatic polynomials
  13. Revision
